Eating Healthy

A healthy diet cannot be achieved without eating garden fresh fruits and vegetables. Following a healthy lifestyle and nutritious diet has been shown to improve general well-being. Eating healthy food keeps people more active and alert. People who regularly include fruits and vegetables in their daily food intake report less or almost no mental health issues as opposed to those prone to eating unhealthy food.

Common myths about dieting

When it comes to overall health and weight loss, there’s an excess of advice out there. Unfortunately, most of it is terrible, misguided, outdated, and scientifically disproven.
Myth 1: Crash dieting or fasting makes you lose weight.
Fact: This may be true in the short term, but ultimately it can hinder weight loss.

  • Myth 2: Low-fat or no-fat diets are good for you.
    Fact: There's no need to follow a fat-free diet. Cutting down on saturated fats and eating unsaturated fats, found in things like olive oil and avocados, will help

    • Diet & Fitness

    If you’re relying on exercise to lose weight without changing your diet, you’re setting yourself up for failure. Regular exercise is important but according to research, nutrition has the largest impact on our fitness. Using food as our medicine has become a popular theme for health improvement.
